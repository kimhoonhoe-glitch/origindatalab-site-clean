Legal & Procurement Overview

License Summary

This page provides a public summary of the baseline licensing structure used for PoC dataset deliveries. Final commercial terms, expanded usage rights, and procurement documents can be shared during review or under NDA when required.

This summary is intended to help engineering, legal, and procurement teams review baseline usage boundaries before detailed agreement review.

At a glance

The standard delivery model is designed for evaluation, internal R&D, and scoped commercial use under non-exclusive terms unless otherwise agreed in writing.

Default model

  • Non-exclusive license
  • Scope-bound usage
  • No resale by default

Best fit

  • Internal evaluation
  • Model testing
  • R&D and PoC programs

Available separately

  • Expanded commercial terms
  • Custom licensing scope
  • NDA-based documentation

Permitted use

The baseline license is generally intended to support internal technical review and approved project workflows without implying unrestricted redistribution or open commercial transfer.

  • Internal evaluation — permitted for model fit-checking, ingestion testing, and workflow validation
  • Research and development — permitted for internal R&D and scenario analysis
  • Benchmarking and testing — permitted for internal technical benchmarking and evaluation experiments
  • PoC usage — permitted within the agreed PoC scope, geography, and delivery structure

Restricted use

Some usage categories are not included by default and require separate written agreement, expanded commercial licensing, or formal procurement review.

  • Unrestricted resale — not permitted by default
  • Redistribution to third parties — not permitted unless explicitly approved in writing
  • Sub-licensing — not included by default
  • Exclusive rights — not included in standard PoC delivery
  • Open-ended production deployment — requires separate commercial scope agreement

Redistribution & resale

The baseline position is simple: delivered dataset materials are intended for the licensed buyer’s internal use within the agreed scope, not for onward sale or uncontrolled transfer.

Generally permitted

  • Internal team access within the licensed organization
  • Internal review by engineering, product, or legal stakeholders
  • Technical integration testing within approved internal workflows

Not permitted by default

  • Resale of raw delivered data
  • Redistribution to affiliates or external parties without agreement
  • Public release or marketplace listing of delivered assets

Internal evaluation & commercial training

Internal technical use is the normal starting point. Broader commercial usage can be supported, but the exact scope must match the procurement and licensing pathway.

  • Internal evaluation — supported as a standard use case
  • Internal model development — generally supported within agreed buyer scope
  • Commercial training programs — may be supported subject to final license scope, commercial terms, and intended deployment model
  • Production scaling — handled separately after PoC validation and agreement review

Where commercial use is broader than baseline PoC evaluation, final permissions should be read from the executed agreement rather than from this public summary alone.

PoC scope

PoC deliveries are intentionally structured to help buyers validate technical fit before any broader production or long-term licensing commitment.

  • Scope-limited delivery — aligned to the agreed PoC tier and review purpose
  • Representative materials — curated clips, metadata, schema preview, and QC summary where applicable
  • No automatic expansion — broader rights or future continuity are not implied without further agreement
  • Procurement handoff — production-scale supply, exclusivity, or custom rights are handled separately after PoC review

Common review questions

Does this page replace the final contract?

No. This page is a public summary only. Final rights, restrictions, and commercial terms are defined in the executed agreement and any related procurement documents.

Can legal or procurement teams review terms before purchase?

Yes. Public summary information is available here, and more detailed materials can be shared during qualification or under NDA where appropriate.

Is commercial use always included?

Not automatically in unlimited form. Commercial usage scope should be confirmed against the final agreement, especially for broader deployment, production use, redistribution, or exclusivity.
